Project Background:


The app, called Walla, is a generosity-driven platform for university students to "holla" out (request) for help, like borrowing a phone charger or jump starting a car. These requests are posted in a feed, which helpers can scroll through. Helpers then select requests they can help with. Think of it as a productive, generosity-powered Yik Yak.

We are about to ship finalized designs to the dev team!

Role:

What started out as a favor for a dear friend turned into an involved project and study on gamifying gratitude and generosity. For this project, I worked as the lead UX Designer at the GenieUs company. My prime responsibility was to lay the groundwork for the mobile app's MVP.

Reflections

  1. Gained a deeper understanding of Material Design and iOS Human Interface guidelines, and the differences between the two.
  2. Android has in-app navigation and default physical/capacitive or on-screen phone navigation, providing more navigational options than what's offered to iOS users. In-app "back" buttons often bring users to the next level up; on-screen Android default back buttons bring users to the page they last accessed. iOS lacks that hierarchical back-jumping.
  3. Horizontal omnipresent navbar for Android. Android tends to use the side drawer for navigation, but as has been discussed many times, there are visibility disadvantages to storing core functions in a hamburger menu. For our first release, we wanted to make the app functions abundantly clear and accessible.
  4. Design decision: when to use cards or full-width flat design? I decided on using cards for important, mutable information. Flat full-width containers were used for things that could not be changed, ie request history.
